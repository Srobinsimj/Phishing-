🚩 Red Flags — Educator Notes

  1. Fake domain: URL is studentaccess.net, not a real university domain.
  2. Padlock is misleading: HTTPS only means the connection is encrypted — it doesn't mean the site is legitimate.
  3. Credential harvesting: Whatever you type is captured — in a real attack it's sent to the attacker silently.
  4. Social login buttons: "Login with Facebook/Google" are fake — used to steal OAuth tokens too.
  5. Professional design: Attackers clone real sites pixel-perfectly to avoid suspicion.
  6. Redirect after login: Victim is sent to the real site — they think they just mistyped their password.
